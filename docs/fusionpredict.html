Important Notes

Real-Time Targets

1 minute means 1 minute. A 5-minute target hits in roughly 5 minutes. Even the 1-hour target typically completes within 45 minutes - not the 10-12 hours a traditional hourly chart spans. Speed varies with volatility.

Multi-Timeframe Alignment

When all 6 timeframes (1m through 1hr) point the same direction, you're at a pivot. These are high-conviction entries - price is far less likely to retrace against you when exhaustion is confirmed across all horizons.

Momentum First

Gap reversion begins once momentum dissipates. If price is hunting liquidity, that move completes before reversion kicks in. Don't fight active momentum - let it exhaust first.

Best Accuracy

Highest accuracy on 1-5 minute charts (97%+). Performance naturally degrades above 15 minutes due to gap-centered logic. Use higher timeframes for directional bias, lower timeframes for precision entries.

How It Works

  • Gap analysis identifies price displacement from fair value across 6 timeframes (1m, 5m, 10m, 15m, 30m, 1hr)
  • Each timeframe displays its predicted target level with directional indicators
  • Green arrows mark bullish targets (price should move up to fill the gap)
  • Red arrows mark bearish targets (price should move down to fill the gap)
  • "ALL LONG" or "ALL SHORT" signals when all 6 timeframes align - these are pivot entries
  • The alignment table shows exact targets for each timeframe plus signal strength
